Crescent

Discovered by William Herschel in 1792, the Crescent Nebula (also known as NGC 6888) is an emission nebula in the constellation Cygnus.

It is formed by the fast stellar wind from the Wolf-Rayet star WR 136 colliding with and energizing the slower wind ejected by WR136 when it became a red giant around 250,000 years ago. The result of the collision is a shell and two shock waves.

Elephant's Trunk

The Elephant's Trunk Nebula is a concentration of interstellar gas and dust within the much larger ionized gas region IC 1396 located in the constellation Cepheus.

It is commonly called the Elephant's Trunk nebula because of its appearance at visible light wavelengths, where there is a dark patch with a bright, sinuous rim. The rim is the surface of the dense cloud that is being illuminated.

Lagoon

Discovered by Giovanni Hodierna before 1654, the Lagoon Nebula (also known as NGC 6523) is a giant interstellar cloud in the constellation Sagittarius. It is an emission nebula that is between 4,000-6,000 light-years away from Earth.

The Lagoon Nebula is one of only two star-forming nebulae faintly visible to the eye from mid-northern latitudes.

Tarantula

The Tarantula Nebula (also known as 30 Doradus) is a diffuse nebula and an H II region in the constellation Dorado. It is located within the Large Magellanic Cloud, which is a satellite galaxy of the Milky Way.

The Tarantula Nebula was observed by Nicolas-Louis de Lacaille during an expedition to the Cape of Good Hope between 1751 and 1753.

Thor's Helmet

Thor's Helmet (also known as NGC 2359) is an emission nebula in the constellation Canis Major. The nebula is approximately 11.96 thousand light years away and 30 light-years in size.

The central star is the Wolf-Rayet star WR7, an extremely hot star thought to be in a brief pre-supernova stage of evolution.

Trifid

The Trifid Nebula (catalogued as Messier 20 or NGC 6514) is an H II region in a star-forming region of the constellation Sagittarius.

Discovered by Charles Messier on June 5, 1764, the Trifid nebula is about 4,100 light years away from Earth. Close-up images show a dense cloud of dust and gas, which is a stellar nursery full of embryonic stars.
