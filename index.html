Crescent

Discovered by William Herschel in 1792, the Crescent Nebula (also known as NGC 6888) is an emission nebula in the constellation Cygnus, about 5000 light-years away from Earth.

The Crescent nebula is formed by the faster stellar wind from the Wolf-Rayet star WR 136 colliding with and energizing the slower moving wind ejected by the star when it became a red giant. The result of the collision is a shell and two shock waves, one moving outward and one inward. The inward moving shock wave heats the stellar wind to X-ray-emitting temperatures.

Elephant's Trunk

The Elephant's Trunk Nebula is a concentration of interstellar gas and dust within the much larger ionized gas region IC 1396 located in the constellation Cepheus about 2,400 light years away from Earth.

The piece of the nebula shown here is the dark, dense globule IC 1396A; it is commonly called the Elephant's Trunk nebula because of its appearance at visible light wavelengths, where there is a dark patch with a bright, sinuous rim. The bright rim is the surface of the dense cloud that is being illuminated and ionized by a very bright, massive star (HD 206267) that is just to the east of IC 1396A.

Lagoon

Discovered by Giovanni Hodierna before 1654, the Lagoon Nebula (also known as NGC 6523) is a giant interstellar cloud in the constellation Sagittarius. It is between 4,000-6,000 light-years away from Earth.

The Lagoon Nebula is one of only two star-forming nebulae faintly visible to the eye from mid-northern latitudes. Seen with binoculars, it appears as a distinct oval cloudlike patch with a definite core. It is classified as an emission nebula and as an H II region, which means it is ionized.

Tarantula

The Tarantula Nebula (also known as 30 Doradus) is a diffuse nebula and an H II region in the Large Magellanic Cloud. It is the most active starburst region known in the Local Group of galaxies.

About 160,000 light years away from Earth, the Tarantula Nebula was observed by Nicolas-Louis de Lacaille during an expedition to the Cape of Good Hope between 1751 and 1753. Located about 160,000 light-years from Earth, its luminosity is so great that if it were as close to Earth as the Orion Nebula, the Tarantula Nebula would cast visible shadows.

Thor's Helmet

NGC 2359 (also known as Thor's Helmet) is an emission nebula in the constellation Canis Major. The nebula is approximately 11.96 thousand light years away and 30 light-years in size.

The central star is the Wolf-Rayet star WR7. Thor's Helmet is similar in nature to the Bubble Nebula, but interactions with a nearby large molecular cloud are thought to have contributed to the more complex shape and curved bow-shock structure. The nebula contains several hundred solar masses of ionised material, plus several thousand more of unionised gas.

Trifid

The Trifid Nebula (catalogued as Messier 20 or NGC 6514) is an H II region in a star-forming region of the Sagittarius Constellation. Discovered by Charles Messier on June 5, 1764, the Trifid nebula is about 4,100 light years away from Earth.

The Trifid Nebula was the subject of an investigation by astronomers. Using the Hubble Space Telescope in 1997, the nebula was observed using filters that isolate emission from hydrogen atoms, ionized sulfur atoms, and doubly ionized oxygen atoms. Close-up images show a dense cloud of dust and gas, which is a stellar nursery full of embryonic stars.
